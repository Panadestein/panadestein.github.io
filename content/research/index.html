At the present moment, I am a postdoctoral fellow within the TREX Project, at the Computational Chemical Physics group of the University of Twente. This European Center of Excellence aims at developing open-source high-performance quantum chemistry software tailored to the emerging exascale architectures. State-of-the-art quantum Monte Carlo algorithms are used for this purpose due to their suitability for massive parallelism. During my time in the project, I have learned and applied innovative programming techniques like the Implicit Reference to Parameters method (IRP).

Formerly I was part of Prof. Daniel Peláez's group, where I worked in the development and application of optimization and tensor decomposition algorithms for obtaining Potential Energy Surfaces that are used in Nuclear Quantum Dynamics calculations with the Multiconfiguration Time-dependent Hartree (MCTDH) method. Here you can find an introductory talk to MCTDH that I have prepared.
